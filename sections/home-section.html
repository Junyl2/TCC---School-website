Talisay City College: A Legacy of Accessible Education

Talisay City College was established in 2004 by the local government of Talisay City, Cebu, through the initiative of then Mayor Eduardo R. Gullas. Born out of a vision to make quality and affordable education accessible to every Talisaynon, especially those from underserved communities, the college was created as a response to the growing demand for higher education in the city and surrounding areas.

Recognizing that many talented students were unable to pursue college due to financial constraints, the city leadership took a bold step in founding a local college. With the passing of a city ordinance, TCC was officially created as a public institution offering degree programs in education, business, and public service.

The college began with modest resources — a handful of classrooms, a small faculty team, and a few pioneering students — but it quickly grew in both size and impact. Over the years, TCC has expanded its academic offerings, improved its facilities, and developed partnerships with other educational institutions. Today, it continues to be a vital force in the community, empowering generations of students through accessible, values-driven education.

Talisay City College stands as a testament to what a community can achieve when it invests in the future of its youth.
